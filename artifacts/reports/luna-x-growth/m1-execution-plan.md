### M1 success criteria
M1 is considered successful only if **all** of the following are true:
1. **Net follower growth:** account reaches **at least 26 followers total**, representing **+25 net followers** from the current baseline of 1.
2. **Follower quality:** at least **60%** of new followers sampled in the M1 review window appear relevant to one or more approved segments from `distribution-map.md` (AI agent builders, technical operators, build-in-public founders, devtools/workflow communities, or evidence-based market structure participants).

6. **Reputational safety:** no sustained negative feedback pattern, platform warning, account restriction, or identity/persona mismatch.

### M1 non-goals
- Maximizing vanity metrics without audience fit
- Using growth hacks, mass engagement, or manipulative tactics
- Expanding into unapproved topics to chase reach
- Public execution before approval gates are cleared

## 3) Phase breakdown

## Phase 1 — Weeks 1–2: Internal prep and evidence hardening

**Goal:** Convert draft strategy into an execution-ready operating package without taking unauthorized public action.

### Exit criteria for Phase 1
- Baseline/snapshot evidence revalidated
- Draft content calendar approved internally
- Target watchlist structure created and populated with first validated candidates
- Draft post/reply templates created for approved pillars
- Approval packet prepared for human review
- No charter conflicts unresolved

## Phase 2 — Weeks 3–4: Controlled engagement rollout

**Goal:** Begin tightly scoped, manually approved public execution with low volume and high review discipline.

### Exit criteria for Phase 2
- “Go live” approval granted by human
- First public post approved and published
- Daily measurement loop operating
- Early engagement quality acceptable
- No guardrail or reputation issues triggered

## Phase 3 — Week 5+: Organic growth optimization

**Goal:** Increase consistency and compounding while staying inside proven formats, segments, and risk limits.

### Exit criteria for Phase 3
- Content cadence sustainable
- Repeatable formats identified
- Follower quality and engagement trends stable or improving
- Account trajectory remains within charter and stop-condition thresholds

## 5) Content calendar template

This is the default M1 template. It remains **draft-only** until go-live approval.

### Weekly frequency template
- **Core posts:** 3 per week
- **Short technical/reactive notes:** 2 per week
- **High-signal replies / quote opportunities:** up to 1–2 per day, only after approval scope exists
- **Weekly review/postmortem:** 1 internal summary, not necessarily public

### Pillar rotation template
Use a controlled rotation so no single theme dominates:
- **Mon:** Pillar A — product/protocol signal & experimentation
- **Tue:** Pillar B — AI-agent workflows / engineering process
- **Wed:** Pillar D — community feedback loop / question-led insight
- **Thu:** Pillar A or B repeat based on available evidence
- **Fri:** Pillar C — trading/automation transparency update (only if evidence-backed and non-promissory)
- **Weekend:** optional low-volume recap or no post, depending on measured quality

### Format mix template
Target format balance per week:
- **40%** short posts / concise observations
- **20%** mini-thread or structured breakdown
- **20%** reaction / quote / contextual commentary
- **20%** question-led or feedback-loop format

### Content slot template
For each draft content item, record:
- Date slot
- Pillar
- Format
- Intended audience segment
- Core claim / observation
- Evidence source
- Risk review result
- Approval state
- Performance notes after posting

## 6) Engagement playbook

The required daily routine is:
**monitor → draft → review → post → measure**

### 1. Monitor
- Review relevant conversations, validated watchlist accounts, and ecosystem signals
- Check whether any topic naturally matches approved pillars
- Avoid engaging with drama, hype, or irrelevant virality

### 2. Draft
- Prepare post/reply candidates tied to one approved pillar
- State the concrete point, evidence basis, and intended audience fit
- Keep tone concise, technical, and candid

### 3. Review
- Run pre-public checklist:
  - pillar fit confirmed
  - no exaggerated claims
  - no policy/charter violation
  - audience fit plausible
  - human approval status clear where required

### 4. Post
- Publish only within approved cadence and approved scope
- Log the content ID/link, time, pillar, and expected hypothesis
- Do not improvise outside plan during early M1

### 5. Measure
- Record initial response window metrics
- Note engagement quality, not just counts
- Compare outcome to hypothesis: who engaged, what resonated, any risk flags

### Daily operating expectation
- Low-volume, high-discipline execution
- No bulk engagement
- No chasing visibility spikes midstream without review

## 8) Risk gates and stop conditions

### Risk gates
The plan must not progress to the next stage unless these gates are satisfied.

#### Gate A — Evidence readiness gate
Required before go-live review:
- Baseline and snapshot artifacts remain consistent
- Pillars and distribution segments are translated into specific drafts/watchlists
- First content bank and review checklist exist

#### Gate B — Human approval gate
Required before any public posting:
- Human explicitly approves moving from planning-only into controlled public execution
- Scope, cadence, and initial content set are approved

#### Gate C — Early quality gate
Required before increasing volume beyond the initial controlled cadence:
- Early posts show acceptable engagement quality
- No meaningful guardrail or reputational issues
- Audience fit is visible, not purely vanity-driven

### Stop conditions
Pause M1 public execution immediately if any of the following occurs:
- Any charter violation or freeze violation
- Platform/session issue or account restriction
- Sudden engagement dominated by spam, bots, or adversarial attention
- Repeated low-fit followers with weak community relevance
- Negative trend in engagement quality over 7-day window
- Content drift into unapproved topics or risky claims
- Human confidence drops due to unclear evidence

## 9) Human approval decision points

The following points require explicit human approval:

1. **Go-live gate**
   - Approval to move from planning/docs into controlled public execution

2. **First public post approval**
   - Exact content reviewed before publication

3. **First public reply/quote-post approval**
   - Especially for reactive engagement with third-party accounts

4. **Cadence expansion approval**
   - Any move from low-volume to higher-frequency posting or replying

5. **Topic expansion approval**
   - Any new topic not already covered by approved pillars and charter constraints

6. **Risk exception approval**
   - Any case where evidence is incomplete but someone proposes proceeding anyway

7. **M1 continuation vs pause decision**
   - Weekly or milestone review when performance is mixed or risk signals rise

## 10) Rollback plan if metrics degrade

If M1 metrics degrade, use the following rollback sequence.

### Trigger conditions for rollback
- Net followers stagnate or reverse materially for 2 consecutive review windows
- Engagement quality declines for 2 consecutive weeks
- Follower-quality sample drops below acceptable threshold
- Posts attract low-trust or off-segment attention
- Content performance weakens after a cadence increase

### Rollback levels

#### Level 1 — Reduce volume
- Cut posting frequency by 30–50%
- Stop experimental formats
- Continue only with highest-confidence pillars

#### Level 2 — Narrow scope
- Suspend reactive engagement
- Pause low-confidence segments from the distribution map
- Return to only 1–2 proven content types

#### Level 3 — Return to planning-only mode
- Pause all public execution
- Re-enter internal review using `day-1-engagement-plan.md` style discipline
- Refresh baseline/snapshot and produce diagnostic review

#### Level 4 — Governance escalation
- Escalate to human with documented failure pattern, candidate causes, and recommended next action: adjust / pause / full stop

### Rollback documentation requirements
Any rollback event must record:
- trigger observed
- date/time
- affected pillar/format/segment
- immediate action taken
- recommendation for restart criteria

## 13) Working conclusion

M1 should be treated as a **gated execution milestone**, not as a raw growth sprint. The evidence set from M0 shows a stable but low-signal starting point: `1 follower / 7 following / session ok`, with no captured recent-post history. Because of that, the safest path to **+25 net followers** is:
- validate the execution package first,
- use a phased rollout,
- require human approval at key transitions,
- measure follower quality alongside growth,
- and roll back quickly if audience fit or risk posture degrades.

This plan intentionally preserves the charter’s governance posture while making M1 operationally actionable once approval is granted.
