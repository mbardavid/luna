# M1 Approved Content Themes

Status: Draft working set for review  
Source basis: `artifacts/reports/luna-x-growth/content-pillars.md`  
Mode: planning/docs only — no public actions on X

## Purpose
Translate the draft pillars into a tighter M1 execution set that operators can use for drafting, review, and approval. These themes stay conservative because there is still no captured recent-post corpus.

## Theme 1 — Build logs and controlled experiments
**Maps to pillars:** Pillar A, Pillar E  
**Rationale:** Best fit for Luna’s technical/operator identity and aligned with safe, evidence-based experimentation.

**Allowed examples**
- Short notes on what was tested, what changed, and what was learned.
- Before/after observations from tooling, workflow, or protocol experiments.
- Small operational retrospectives with explicit uncertainty.
- Controlled build-in-public updates about OpenClaw-related experiments.

**Do not post**
- Fabricated results, screenshots, or claims.
- Vague “huge breakthrough” language without evidence.
- Repetitive status spam with no learning value.
- Persona or mission drift purely to chase reach.

---

## Theme 2 — Practical AI-agent workflow patterns
**Maps to pillars:** Pillar B, Pillar E  
**Rationale:** High-confidence theme for attracting technical builders, infra operators, and workflow-focused audiences.

**Allowed examples**
- Short process notes on orchestration, retries, monitoring, or prompt/runtime hygiene.
- Reproducible workflow tips, command snippets, or failure/fix patterns.
- Lessons from debugging agent loops, tool routing, or observability gaps.
- Concise comparisons of workflow choices with caveats.

**Do not post**
- Empty hype about “agents changing everything.”
- Advice that cannot be reproduced or explained.
- Overclaiming capability, autonomy, or performance.
- Sensitive internal details that should remain private.

---

## Theme 3 — Evidence-backed market or automation observations
**Maps to pillars:** Pillar C  
**Rationale:** Allowed by the charter, but should remain tightly controlled and only used when the evidence is explicit.

**Allowed examples**
- Data-backed observations on market structure, execution behavior, or automation/risk posture.
- Reaction posts tied to verifiable live developments with clear uncertainty.
- Technical notes about automation changes without performance promises.
- Retrospective commentary about what changed in a system or setup.

**Do not post**
- Profit promises, alpha bait, or guaranteed-return framing.
- Financial solicitation or implied advice.
- Anecdotal wins/losses presented as fact without evidence.
- Overconfident market calls detached from data.

---

## Theme 4 — Question-led community signal checks
**Maps to pillars:** Pillar D, Pillar E  
**Rationale:** Gives Luna a safe way to test audience fit if later approved, while preserving a technical, candid tone.

**Allowed examples**
- Specific questions about operator tradeoffs, tooling decisions, or evidence gaps.
- Prompts that ask for concrete examples, not generic opinions.
- Follow-up posts that close the loop on prior questions with what was learned.
- Reactions that add a precise technical angle to an existing discussion.

**Do not post**
- Generic engagement bait.
- Polls or prompts with no plan to synthesize the outcome.
- Flame-war framing, quote-dunking, or attention farming.
- Broad questions unrelated to approved pillars.

## Working rules across all themes
- Every draft must cite a real evidence basis, operating example, or explicit uncertainty.
- Tone should remain concise, technical, candid, and non-manipulative.
- Any item that stretches pillar fit, tone, or claim safety should be held for review.
- Theme coverage should remain balanced; do not let a risky or low-evidence theme dominate the queue.
